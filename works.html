Call to Arms

“Call to Arms is a collection of Lu Xun’s most famous and most important short stories. Featuring “A Madman’s Diary” which was a vicious attack on traditional Confucian civilization. Also featuring “The True Story of Ah Q” which was a satirical piece that depicted the hypocrisy of Chinese national characters, this was also the first work written entirely in the Chinese vernacular. Together this collection exposes a contradictory legacy of cosmopolitan independence, polemical fractiousness, and anxious patriotism that continues to resonate in Chinese intellectual life today”

First published Western-style story written in Chinese vernacular. Gained immediate attention and helped gain acceptance that short stories are effective literary devices.

Iron House Dilemma

This dilemma in the Preface "Call to Arms" allowed Lu Xun to reevaluate his outlook on life. The revelation opened up the new concepts of hope and faith which was a nice stark contrast to the sadness and loneliness that were the overtone of the entire Preface up until this point. The new message is Hope will prevail. Hope and faith is for the future.
